The New York Sun is returning as the voice of principled, authoritative journalism in an era of record distrust in media and institutional decline.

Credited as “one of the most influential American newspapers,” the Sun was founded in 1833 and ran for over 100 years as a Pulitzer-winning independent daily until its merger with The New York World-Telegram in 1950. In 2002, the Sun was relaunched under the leadership of Editor-in-Chief Seth Lipsky and competed with The New York Times. In 2022, the Sun returns to reclaim its historic role.
